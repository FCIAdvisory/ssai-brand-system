The star that

A single ordinary star, 93 million miles away, holding eight planets in its grip and pouring out the light that makes life possible.

The Sun, up close

A surface in
constant revolt.

Plasma at 5,500°C churns and boils. Magnetic fields knot into active regions and snap, flinging flares across the void in minutes.

An eruptive prominence

When the
field gives way.

Cooler clouds of plasma hang suspended above the limb on magnetic loops — until the field reconnects and hurls them into space.

The corona

A million-degree
crown.

The Sun's outer atmosphere burns hundreds of times hotter than the surface below it — and it is the corona that launches the solar wind.

Weather that
reaches Earth.

A continuous stream of charged particles fills the heliosphere and slams into Earth's magnetic field — disrupting satellites, signals and power grids.
