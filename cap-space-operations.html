Space Operations

We keep the mission running, around the clock.

From deep space to space weather, we operate the models, instruments, and mission systems the science depends on. We support NOAA and NASA weather and climate missions 24/7, develop and calibrate instruments bound for distant worlds, and run integrated operations from pre-launch testing through on-orbit, when the mission cannot fail.

The Capability

We operate the systems the mission runs on.

We deliver continuous, mission-critical space operations across the full arc of a mission, from the modeling that frames the science to the operations that keep spacecraft on station and returning data.

We operate NASA's GEOS Data Assimilation System, ingesting millions of satellite and ground observations every day and advancing the global atmospheric reanalysis used by researchers worldwide. We sustain that work around the clock, providing 24/7 operational modeling support for NOAA and NASA weather and climate missions. The same discipline carries to deep space, where we develop and calibrate instruments, design trajectories, and model atmospheric entry for planetary missions.

We serve the agencies that operate in space and depend on it: NASA and NOAA mission directorates, the defense and national security communities that rely on assured access to space, and the science teams whose missions cannot afford an interruption in operations or data.

What We Do

From the model to the mission.

We frame each discipline by the outcome it secures, and we name the work that delivers it. This is operations measured by whether the data comes back, and the mission stays on course.

01 Data assimilation

We turn observations into a coherent picture of the planet

We operate NASA's GEOS Data Assimilation System, ingesting millions of satellite and ground observations daily and advancing the global atmospheric reanalysis used by researchers worldwide.

02 Earth system modeling

We model the planet as a single, coupled system

We develop next-generation coupled atmosphere, ocean, land, and chemistry models, advancing the science that drives weather and climate prediction across NOAA and NASA.

03 Operational support

We sustain the modeling the mission cannot pause

We provide 24/7 operational modeling support for NOAA and NASA weather and climate missions, so forecasts and products reach the people who depend on them, on time, every cycle.

04 Instrument operations

We ready and calibrate instruments bound for deep space

We develop deep-space instruments and calibrate them for the conditions they will meet, so the measurements they return are trusted from the first observation.

05 Trajectory & entry

We design the path and model the arrival

We perform trajectory design and atmospheric-entry modeling for planetary missions, charting the route to the target and the physics of reaching its surface.

06 Mission operations

We run operations from pre-launch through on-orbit

We deliver integrated mission operations from pre-launch testing through on-orbit, alongside science data processing, archiving, and distribution that puts every measurement in reach.

Proven

Operations the agencies rely on.

Our work runs on the systems that science agencies trust and on the flagship missions that define a generation of exploration. We extend the same atmospheric modeling that measures Earth to Mars, Titan, and other planetary environments.

  • GEOS Data Assimilation System. We operate NASA's GEOS Data Assimilation System, ingesting millions of satellite and ground observations daily and advancing the global atmospheric reanalysis used worldwide.
  • 24/7 NOAA and NASA modeling. We sustain continuous operational modeling support for NOAA and NASA weather and climate missions, with no break in the cycle.
  • Coupled Earth system models. We develop next-generation coupled atmosphere, ocean, land, and chemistry models for weather and climate prediction.
  • Deep-space instruments. We develop and calibrate instruments for deep-space missions and design the trajectories and entry profiles that carry them to their targets.
  • Science data lifecycle. We process, archive, and distribute science data, and run integrated mission operations from pre-launch testing through on-orbit.
  • Beyond Earth. We extend Earth atmospheric modeling to Mars, Titan, and other planetary environments, applying terrestrial science to distant worlds.
