Science · Data Science & AI

From raw signal to trusted knowledge.

A satellite returns a stream of bits. Turning that stream into a temperature field, a chlorophyll map, or a forty-year climate record takes algorithms, assimilation, and machines that never sleep. SSAI builds that layer of the science enterprise, the data assimilation, AI and machine learning, modeling, high-performance computing, archives, and visualization that move an observation from orbit to understanding.

Explore the stack Mission Ops & Data

The science between the satellite and the answer.

For nearly fifty years SSAI has worked at the point where measurement becomes meaning. Our scientists and computer scientists develop the retrieval algorithms that convert sensor counts into geophysical quantities, the assimilation systems that fuse millions of observations into coherent global fields, and the reanalyses that stitch decades of disparate instruments into a single continuous record.

We do this across all three of our science domains, the Sun, the Earth, and the universe beyond, but the gravity sits in Earth science, where the volume of observation is largest and the demand for trustworthy, well-characterized data is most relentless. Every product we deliver carries calibration, validation, and uncertainty alongside it, because a number a policymaker or a forecaster acts on is only as good as the work behind it.

The capability stack

Six disciplines, one pipeline.

Observation rarely arrives ready to use. These are the disciplines SSAI brings to bear, in roughly the order signal travels, from a raw downlink to a finished, citable result a scientist trusts.

01 · Fusion

Data Assimilation

We blend satellite, airborne, and in-situ observations with physical models to produce the best estimate of a system’s true state. SSAI supports NASA’s Global Modeling and Assimilation Office, where our team contributed to the model–data fusion behind MERRA-2.

Earth as a dataset

A planet, assimilated.

Every point on this globe is the meeting place of countless observations, a swath here, a sounding there, a buoy, a balloon, a model background. Data assimilation reconciles them into one coherent picture of the Earth at a moment in time, then steps that picture forward. It is how a chaotic shower of measurements becomes a forecast, a reanalysis, a record we can trust.

Seeing the data

Scientific visualization.

Petabyte-scale simulations and global imagery are useless if no one can see them. SSAI develops immersive scientific visualization and the software that puts data in front of human eyes, from interactive model rendering to the visualization walls where scientists explore a result together.

Our development teams are behind some of NASA’s most visible science software: Worldview and the Global Imagery Browse Services (GIBS) put global satellite imagery in the hands of millions, while FLUID, GMAO’s Framework for Live User-Invoked Data, drives interactive model visualization.

  • Worldview & GIBS, browseable global imagery at planetary scale
  • FLUID, live, interactive model-output visualization for GMAO
  • Immersive visualization for data-exploration theaters and hyperwalls

Machines for science

Compute & exploration.

The questions get bigger; so do the data. SSAI runs the global mesoscale and multi-scale modeling that resolves weather inside climate, and manages the high-performance computing and networking that science at this resolution requires, the unglamorous backbone without which none of the rest runs.

On the hyperwall and in the data-exploration theater, that compute becomes something a scientist can reach into, a galaxy survey, a hurricane reanalysis, a decade of sea ice, explored at full resolution rather than reduced to a thumbnail. It is where the pipeline closes the loop, returning understanding to the people who asked the question.

Software & platforms

Named software, real reach.

Data science at SSAI is not abstract. It ships as software with users, the platforms and archives that carry NASA and NOAA science out of the lab and into operations, classrooms, and the public square.

Why it can be trusted.

Anyone can compute a number. The hard part, and the part SSAI is built for, is computing one that holds up. Our pipelines carry provenance, metadata, and uncertainty as first-class outputs, not afterthoughts. We define the product formats and metadata templates that make a dataset findable and reusable years after its mission ends, and we validate against independent measurements before anything is called a product.

That discipline is institutional. SSAI leads the agency-wide Earth Science Data System Working Groups, holds CMMI Level 3 and ISO 9001:2015, and earns the formal authorizations, like the STARSS Authorization to Operate, that let our systems run on national science networks. The result is data scientists cite, forecasters act on, and the public can see.
