The Science of SSAI

From observation to understanding

For more than forty years, SSAI has invested in the science behind Earth's complex systems and our place in the universe. We organize that work around three domains — the Earth, the Sun, and the universe beyond — designing and maintaining the instruments that observe each, and managing the data they return.

Science is the work, not the byproduct

Whether designing, building, maintaining, or upgrading ground- and space-based instruments — or managing, analyzing, and distributing the data they return — SSAI contributes to advances across the oceanic, terrestrial, atmospheric, biological, and astronomical sciences.

Domain 01 — The Earth

Earth Science

Our largest practice.

SSAI scientists contribute to nearly every NASA observing system measuring Earth's atmosphere, water, ice, land, and biological activity. Our work spans remote sensing — from algorithm development through data validation, calibration, maintenance, and distribution.

We observe and model Earth-system components such as land surface and hydrology, the oceans, and the cryosphere, alongside geodesy and solid-Earth geophysics. We develop algorithms that detect and quantify water-related variables from satellite, airborne, and in-situ data, applying large-scale geospatial processing and high-performance computing to atmospheric correction and aquatic applications.

Domain 02 — The Sun

Heliophysics

SSAI studies the interaction of the solar wind with the upper atmosphere and the science of space weather — understanding and predicting solar activity and its interaction with Earth's magnetic field.

We support the development and operations of NAIRAS — the Nowcast for Aerospace Ionizing Radiation System, developed at NASA Langley — which computes in real time the radiation experienced by the crews of aircraft and spacecraft from galactic cosmic rays, solar energetic particles, and the radiation belts. We also study variations in total solar irradiance as a fundamental forcing of Earth's climate system.

Domain 03 — The Universe

Planetary Science & Astrophysics

SSAI extends its science from the Moon to the edge of the observable universe. We are developing HOLMS — the Heterodyne OH Lunar Miniaturized Spectrometer — destined for the lunar surface through NASA's Commercial Lunar Payload Services initiative, to measure and understand the water cycle in the Moon's thin atmosphere.

That water will be crucial to Artemis, which aims to establish a permanent human presence on the Moon. Our instrument and engineering teams reach further still, into the astrophysics missions that map our place among the stars.
