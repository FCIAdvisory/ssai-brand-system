The Science Practice

Five decades of mission science.

SSAI's science heritage spans the full reach of observation: Earth, atmosphere, oceans, ice, heliophysics, and planetary and astrophysical science. We have built that depth alongside NASA Goddard, including the science divisions of Code 600, for more than four decades.

We work the entire chain from measurement to meaning. We engineer and calibrate the instruments, operate the missions, and assimilate the observations into Earth-system and physical models. We apply remote sensing, numerical modeling, and data assimilation to turn sensor signals into validated science data products, and we enrich those products with AI/ML so patterns surface faster and forecasts sharpen.

This is the practice the federal and defense science enterprise relies on to measure a changing planet, forecast the space environment, and answer questions that cannot be answered any other way. We turn observation into understanding, and understanding into mission advantage.

Inside the Practice

Five sub-capabilities, one discipline.

The Science practice is organized into five sub-capabilities. Each is grounded in named programs and the scientists who run them, and each is procurable on its own or as part of a larger mission.

Practice Leadership

A single accountable authority.

SSAI is appointing a dedicated Practice Lead for the Science practice. The appointed lead will bring named program depth across Earth, atmosphere, heliophysics, and planetary science, and will serve as the single accountable authority for delivery, from proposal through operations. This is the person agencies will hold responsible for the science, the schedule, and the result, the first time and every time. This block is reserved for that appointment and will carry the lead's name and background once confirmed.
