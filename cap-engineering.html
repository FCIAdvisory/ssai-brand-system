The Capability

We turn requirements into qualified hardware.

SSAI delivers full-lifecycle science, technology, and engineering services for systems that have to work the first time, and every time. We carry a design from concept through requirements, build, integration, test, and verification, and we put our name on the flight unit that comes out the other end.

We serve the programs where there is no second chance. NASA and NOAA flagship instruments, defense and national security hardware, and the spacecraft systems that operate in space and cannot be recalled to the bench. We hold the standards that govern that work, and we apply the same discipline to every customer we serve.

We are AS9100 certified, CMMI Level 3 appraised for software, ITAR registered, and ANSI S20.20 compliant for ESD control. Our workmanship is held to ANSI/IPC-J-STD-001F and its Space Addendum. The credentials are not the work, but they are the proof that the work meets the standard the mission demands.

What We Do

Six disciplines, one qualified system.

We do not sell tasks. We deliver outcomes, and each one rests on named engineering disciplines we own end to end. This is the work that takes a flight system from a requirement document to a unit that passes acceptance and performs on orbit.

01 Outcome

A system that meets the requirement

We define what the system must do, then prove it does. Systems engineering, requirements development, and verification and validation hold the design accountable to the mission from concept through acceptance.

02 Outcome

Avionics that fly

We design the electronics that command and control the spacecraft. Avionics systems design covers the flight hardware that has to operate through launch, vacuum, and the radiation environment without a service call.

03 Outcome

Software the mission can trust

We write flight and ground software under an appraised process. Our software engineering is CMMI Level 3 appraised, so the code that runs the instrument is built to a repeatable, measured standard.

04 Outcome

Logic built into the silicon

We implement mission logic in reconfigurable hardware. FPGA design lets us put timing-critical and high-reliability functions directly into the device, where performance and determinism matter most.

05 Outcome

Parts that survive the mission

We qualify the components before they ever reach the board. Electrical, electronic, and electromechanical (EEE) parts processing screens, selects, and traces every part to the reliability the environment demands.

06 Outcome

Hardware proven before it ships

We build it and we prove it. System integration and testing, supported by manufacturing engineering, assembles the flight unit and qualifies it under environmental and acceptance test before it leaves our hands.

  • Systems engineering Requirements development, verification, and validation across the full lifecycle.
  • Avionics systems design Flight electronics for command, control, and instrument operation.
  • Software engineering Flight and ground software developed under a CMMI Level 3 appraised process.
  • FPGA design Reconfigurable logic for timing-critical and high-reliability functions.
  • EEE parts processing Screening, selection, and traceability for electrical, electronic, and electromechanical parts.
  • Integration & testing Assembly, environmental test, and acceptance, supported by manufacturing engineering.

Held to standard

The standards behind the flight unit.

Every flagship runs on workmanship that can be inspected and proven. We build to ANSI/IPC-J-STD-001F and its Space Addendum, inspect to IPC-610, and rework to IPC-7711/7721. We operate AS9100 certified and ITAR registered, with ESD control to ANSI S20.20.

  • J-STD-001F & Space Addendum Soldered electrical and electronic assembly workmanship.
  • IPC-610 Acceptability of electronic assemblies.
  • IPC-7711/7721 Rework, modification, and repair of assemblies.
  • AS9100 & ITAR Aerospace quality management and export-controlled registration.
  • ANSI S20.20 Electrostatic discharge control program.
