We secure the public-sector mission, end to end.

We protect the networks, data, and systems that agencies cannot afford to lose. CYBERSAFE is our end-to-end framework for public-sector cyber, nine disciplines that carry a mission from risk assessment to authorization to 24/7 defense, so it holds the first time, and every time.

The CYBERSAFE Framework

One framework, the full cyber lifecycle.

CYBERSAFE is SSAI's end-to-end cybersecurity framework for the public sector. It is a single, disciplined approach that moves an agency from understanding its risk, to building defensible architecture, to operating under continuous monitoring and authorization.

We build on the standards federal missions are held to. We deliver the NIST Risk Management Framework and Zero Trust, stand up architectures that earn FedRAMP, FISMA, and HIPAA compliance, and run 24/7 security operations with SIEM and XDR. We prepare the System Security Plan and the POA&M that carry a system to authorization to operate, then we keep defending it.

The framework is built for agencies whose systems cannot fail. We secure the mission against the threat that exists today, and we forecast the one that is coming, so the people who depend on these systems stay protected and ahead.

CYBERSAFE, Component by Component

Nine disciplines, spelled CYBERSAFE.

Each letter of CYBERSAFE is a discipline we own, ordered the way a mission actually moves: from knowing what to protect, to defending it, to staying ahead of what comes next.

C

Critical Asset Identification

We map the systems, data, and dependencies that matter most, so protection is aimed at what the mission cannot lose. The foundation of NIST RMF system categorization.

Y

Yielding Risk Intelligence

We assess threats, vulnerabilities, and impact to produce a clear, prioritized read on risk. Risk intelligence agency leaders can act on, not a list they have to decode.

B

Building Secure Architecture

We design defensible systems on Zero Trust principles, where no user, device, or process is trusted by default. Identity, segmentation, and continuous verification engineered in.

E

Enforce Policy & Compliance

We align systems to the standards federal missions are held to: NIST 800-53, FedRAMP, FISMA, and HIPAA. Controls implemented, documented, and ready for audit.

R

Responding & Recovering Swiftly

We stand up incident response and recovery so an event becomes a contained event, not a mission failure. Playbooks, drills, and the coordination to execute under pressure.

S

Securing the Human Layer

We harden the layer adversaries target first: people. Phishing-resistant access, security awareness, and insider-threat practices that close the gap social engineering exploits.

A

Analyzing & Improving Continuously

We run continuous monitoring with SIEM and XDR, then feed what we learn back into the architecture. Security that measures itself and gets stronger over time.

F

Forecast Emerging Threats

We watch the threat that is coming, not just the one that landed. Threat intelligence and red teaming that test the system against the adversary before the adversary does.

E

Engaging Stakeholders Transparently

We keep the agency, its authorizing officials, and its partners informed with clear reporting. Transparent posture that earns trust and keeps authorization current.

Services

What CYBERSAFE delivers in practice.

Authorization & compliance

From risk to ATO, documented and defensible

We run the NIST Risk Management Framework end to end and prepare agencies for authorization to operate. We author the System Security Plan and maintain the POA&M, aligning systems to FedRAMP, FISMA, and HIPAA so authorizing officials can sign with confidence.

  • NIST RMF & Zero Trust. Categorize, select, implement, assess, authorize, and monitor, built on a zero-trust foundation.
  • ATO readiness. SSP authoring, POA&M management, and the artifacts that carry a system to authorization.
  • FedRAMP, FISMA & HIPAA. Controls mapped, implemented, and evidenced for the frameworks federal missions answer to.
  • 24/7 SOC with SIEM & XDR. Round-the-clock monitoring, detection, and response across the enterprise.
  • Red teaming. Adversary-emulation testing that finds the gap before an adversary can.
  • CISA & MS-ISAC integration. Threat feeds and coordinated defense wired into national cyber channels.
