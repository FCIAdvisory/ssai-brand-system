01 / Who we are

A science company whose laboratory is space.

In 1977, Dr. Om and Sara Bahethi founded SSAI on a single conviction: that knowledge has the power to transform lives. From a one‑person beginning it grew — organically, deliberately — into a partner that has stood beside NASA across more than 150 Earth and space science missions.

Today SSAI is a family‑led, woman‑owned company of scientists, engineers, and data analysts embedded in nearly every laboratory of NASA Goddard's Earth Science Division and at centers nationwide — still guided by a spirit of exploration, respect for our planet, and a desire to leave it better than we found it.

08 / Verbal style

How we write the details.

A house style built on AP, tuned for science. Consistency is its own form of credibility.

Style‑01
Acronyms

Spell out on first use with the acronym in parentheses — except universally known ones. NASA needs none; GSFC, NOAA, USDA do.

Style‑02
The company name

First reference: Science Systems and Applications, Inc. (SSAI). After that, SSAI. Never "Scientific Systems."

Style‑03
Oxford comma

Always. "The Sun, the Earth, and the universe."

Style‑04
Numbers

Spell out one through nine; figures for 10 and up. Always figures with units, ages, and data.

Style‑05
Capitalized science

Capitalize formal mission and instrument names (Black Marble, MERRA‑2). Lowercase generic fields (atmospheric science).

Style‑06
Place the agency

Locate organizations on first reference so non‑experts can follow — "the Atmospheric Science Data Center at NASA Langley."

Style‑07
One space

One space between sentences, everywhere.

Style‑08
Honorifics

Use "Dr." for all doctorates on every reference. For officials, give title and jurisdiction; skip party unless essential.
