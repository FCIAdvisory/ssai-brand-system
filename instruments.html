The instruments we help fly

The people who make instruments succeed.

Our Electrical Systems Engineering Services team — more than 180 engineers and technicians supporting NASA Goddard and other centers — fills the roles missions depend on. We are not the satellite prime and we do not own the flight hardware; we are the engineering, integration, operations, and data discipline that carries it from a study concept to finished, validated science.

On any given program you will find SSAI staff in the seats that decide whether an instrument flies: Integration & Test Manager, Instrument Systems Engineer, Instrument Manager, subsystem leads for power, communications, and command-and-data-handling, and Deputy Mission Operations Manager.

What we do with an instrument

Engineer. Integrate. Operate. Turn into data.

Every instrument in our portfolio passed through some combination of these hands. We frame our role in the verbs we can stand behind — the flight hardware belongs to NASA, NOAA, and their primes; the engineering, integration, operations, and data pipelines that make it work are ours.

01 — Engineer

Engineer

Parts engineering, electronics, harness, and subsystem design for instruments that get one chance to perform. We carry hardware from a study concept through fabrication.

02 — Integrate & Test

Integrate & Test

Integration and test management, verification, and the environmental gauntlet that proves a sensor will survive launch and operate on orbit exactly as specified.

03 — Manage & Operate

Manage & Operate

Instrument and mission management seats, plus payload operations centers we build and run — commanding instruments and keeping them healthy across years on orbit.

04 — Turn Into Data

Turn Into Data

The step that makes the rest matter: raw sensor counts processed, calibrated, formatted, archived, and distributed as finished science products the world can use.

Portfolio · 02

Weather & space-weather.

From geostationary orbit and the L1 point, these instruments watch storms, lightning, the Sun, and the solar wind. SSAI ran Integration & Test across the GOES-R series and continues to support operations from NOAA's Satellite Operations Facility.

GOES-R / S / T / U · I&T

GOES-R Suite

SSAI ran Integration & Test across the GOES-R series — GLM, SUVI, EXIS, SEISS, CCOR, and the Magnetometer. GOES-U launched June 25, 2024; SSAI continues to support GOES operations from NOAA's Satellite Operations Facility.

On-orbit · operating
JPSS · Operational Series

VIIRS

The imaging radiometer that anchors the U.S. operational weather record — day/night imagery, sea-surface temperature, and active fire detection across the JPSS satellites.

On-orbit
L1 Point · Solar Wind

DSCOVR

Deep-space observation of Earth and the solar wind from the L1 Lagrange point — a vantage that delivers both full-disk Earth imagery and space-weather early warning.

On-orbit
  • GLM — Geostationary Lightning Mapper, tracking total lightning across the hemisphere.
  • SUVI — Solar Ultraviolet Imager, watching the Sun for flares and eruptions.
  • EXIS — Extreme Ultraviolet and X-ray Irradiance Sensors.
  • SEISS — Space Environment In-Situ Suite, plus the CCOR Compact Coronagraph and the GOES Magnetometer.
