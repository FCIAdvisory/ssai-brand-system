The Capability

From raw observation to actionable intelligence.

A sensor produces a signal. A decision requires meaning. We close that gap. We take what instruments measure and turn it into geophysical products, forecasts, and intelligence that planners and operators can use.

We work the full chain: calibrate the measurement, retrieve the geophysical quantity, assimilate it into the model, and deliver the product that answers the question. Our scientists and engineers span remote sensing, data assimilation, oceanography, hydrology, biosphere modeling, and weather and space-weather prediction, the disciplines that turn the view from space into understanding on the ground.

We serve the agencies and planners who depend on environmental truth: climate and air-quality programs, disaster-response operations, and the ISR-adjacent missions where knowing the environment first is decision advantage. When the answer has to be right, and on time, this is the work we do.

What We Do

The disciplines behind the answer.

Environmental intelligence is not one method. It is a stack of disciplines, each turning a different kind of observation into a different kind of certainty. We field them together, so the product that reaches the decision maker carries the full weight of the science behind it.

01 Remote Sensing

We see what the surface cannot show

We retrieve geophysical quantities from space-based and airborne sensors: ozone and trace gases, aerosols, clouds, ocean color, vegetation, ice, and surface water. The discipline turns radiance into measurement, and measurement into a product the mission can use.

02 Data Assimilation

We fuse observation into the model

We blend disparate observations with physical models to produce the most accurate possible state of the system. Data assimilation is how scattered measurements become a coherent, gridded picture of the atmosphere, ocean, and land at any moment in time.

03 Oceanography

We read the ocean as a system

We characterize sea surface height, circulation, temperature, and ocean color, connecting physical and biological state. Ocean intelligence underwrites maritime operations, fisheries and climate science, and the long view of a warming planet.

04 Hydrology

We track water across the cycle

We measure surface water, soil moisture, and the movement of water through the landscape. Hydrologic intelligence drives flood and drought response, water-resource planning, and the trafficability assessments operations depend on.

05 Biosphere Modeling

We quantify the living surface

We model vegetation, land cover, carbon, and ecosystem change from orbit. Biosphere intelligence informs climate accounting, agricultural and forest monitoring, and the environmental baselines that long-horizon planning requires.

06 Weather Prediction

We forecast the atmosphere and beyond

We support numerical weather forecasting and prediction, and we model space weather, the solar-driven conditions that disturb satellites, communications, and the grid. Both turn observation into warning before the event arrives.

  • Observing System Simulation Experiments (OSSE). Before a sensor flies, we model what it would see. We run OSSEs to quantify the impact a proposed instrument or constellation would have on forecast skill, so agencies invest in the observations that move the answer.
  • Calibration and validation. We anchor every product to ground truth, so the intelligence the mission acts on is traceable, defensible, and right.
  • Operational delivery. We move the science from research into round-the-clock production, where forecasts and products reach the people who use them on the schedule the mission sets.
Proven

The instruments behind the intelligence.

Our environmental intelligence is built on real sensors, real retrievals, and real operational products. We support the instruments that measure air quality, ocean color, clouds, aerosols, vegetation, and surface elevation, across orbit and in the air.

Air Quality & Composition

Atmosphere

OMI and OMPS for ozone and trace gases, TROPOMI for high-resolution air-quality composition, and GEMS for geostationary air-quality monitoring. Together they track the air people breathe and the chemistry that governs it.

Clouds & Aerosols

Atmosphere

CATS-ISS and CPL (Cloud Physics Lidar) for vertical profiles of clouds and aerosols, MISR for multi-angle aerosol and surface retrievals, and EPIC for full-disk Earth imaging from deep space.

Land, Ocean & Surface

Biosphere & Cryosphere

MODIS and VIIRS for ocean color, land surface, fire, and vegetation, AVIRIS-NG for airborne imaging spectroscopy, and ICESat-2 for precise surface and ice elevation.

The full portfolio

OMI, OMPS, CATS-ISS, MODIS, VIIRS, MISR, EPIC, CPL, ICESat-2, AVIRIS-NG, TROPOMI, and GEMS. We turn each one into validated geophysical products and the intelligence built on top of them.

In Practice

When the environment is the mission.

Environmental intelligence proves itself when a decision cannot wait. We turn observation into the warning, the assessment, and the situational picture that operations act on.

  • Climate. We deliver the long-baseline records and assimilated states that let agencies measure a changing planet and hold the science to account.
  • Air quality. We turn space-based composition measurements into the products that track pollution, smoke, and hazardous plumes across borders and at city scale.
  • Disaster response. We support the hurricane, flood, fire, and dust-storm intelligence that responders depend on, observation turned into warning before and during the event.
  • Decision advantage. We provide the ISR-adjacent environmental picture, weather, ocean, and surface conditions, that gives operators the edge of knowing the environment first.
