01 — Operate

Mission operations that don't sleep

SSAI staff manage integration and test, payload activation, and on-orbit testing for national science missions. Our engineers fill roles including Integration & Test Manager and Deputy Mission Operations Manager — supporting orbit-raising, payload activation, and the daily work of keeping instruments healthy in space.

That includes the GOES program at NOAA's Satellite Operations Facility in Suitland, Maryland, where the difference between a healthy instrument and a lost one is measured in vigilance, not luck.

  • Integration & test management for national science missions
  • Payload activation and on-orbit testing
  • Orbit-raising and early-operations support
  • Deputy Mission Operations Manager and Integration & Test Manager roles
  • GOES program support at NOAA's Satellite Operations Facility, Suitland, MD
02 — Archive

Data systems & DAACs

SSAI operates the Atmospheric Science Data Center (ASDC) Distributed Active Archive Center at NASA Langley for the TEMPO and MAIA missions — defining data-product formats and metadata templates and running the full ingest, archive, and distribution pipeline.

We pioneered direct-broadcast processing for OMI and OMPS, the first Goddard package to take raw sensor data all the way through to finished products, and we disseminate NASA data through portals including the LAADS DAAC.

04 — Learn

AI / machine learning

SSAI runs a dedicated computer-science practice advancing artificial intelligence and machine learning for Earth and space science — and leads the agency-wide Earth Science Data System Working Groups (ESDSWG).

Our work includes machine-learning models that retrieve ocean chlorophyll from OMI ultraviolet and visible measurements, matching established sensors with improved spatial coverage, and accurate satellite retrievals across TROPOMI, GEMS, PACE, and TEMPO.

05 / 06 — Model & Compute

Modeling, simulation & high-performance computing

SSAI supports NASA's Global Modeling and Assimilation Office, where our team contributed to MERRA-2 — a model–data fusion reanalysis providing a continuous atmospheric record since 1980, recognized with a 2019 NASA Agency Honor Award (Group Achievement).

We develop immersive scientific visualization, run global mesoscale and multi-scale modeling, and manage the high-performance computing and networking that science at this scale demands — earning NASA Langley Authorization to Operate for the STARSS Science Support Network.
